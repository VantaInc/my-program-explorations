The scenario

Meridian has three core products — Forge (dev tools), Atlas (project management), and Orbit (cloud storage). Each operating as its own business unit with distinct customer bases, risk profiles, and compliance obligations. All three need SOC 2 Type II re-certifications within the same 90-day window. Atlas is also pursuing ISO 27001.

The previous audit season was a grind: spreadsheets everywhere, constant Slack fire drills, last-minute evidence scrambles. This time, Pam has Vanta.

⚡ Stakes — Meridian's largest-ever enterprise deal requires annual attestation across all three products. One slipped deadline puts the contract at risk.

Monday — Day One

The Setup: Getting Pam Up to Speed

It's Monday. Pam got handed the three programs on Friday. She opens Vanta for the first time as their owner.

Forge and Atlas have existing Vanta data from the previous program manager. Orbit was configured manually last year and is a mess — stale integrations, outdated evidence, unclear ownership. Rather than handing Pam a blank slate or a wall of red, Vanta kicks off an onboarding assessment. It analyzes all three programs, surfaces what's connected versus what's missing, and presents a prioritized starting point.

Vanta AI — Program Assessment
"Forge and Atlas are in good shape — 87% of controls have evidence from the last 90 days. Orbit has 14 integrations listed, but 6 are stale and 3 have never pulled evidence. Here's what I'd fix first."

Pam reviews the recommendations, approves the ones that look right, and flags one for a closer look. Vanta queues up cleanup tasks and assigns the stale Orbit items to Denise. Within 20 minutes, Pam knows exactly where she's starting from. Not from a wall of controls — from a clear picture of what needs her and what doesn't.

Day-to-Day: The Wrench

A procurement contact from the financial services customer emails Pam: "Can you confirm Atlas's current SOC 2 report and ISO 27001 timeline? Our legal team needs it by Friday."

Pam opens Atlas's program. The prior SOC 2 report is live on the Trust Center, but the new one isn't ready. ISO 27001 track is at 76% complete. Vanta immediately surfaces the blockers — not a list of controls, but a theme-level read on what's holding things up.

Vanta AI — Blocker Summary
"ISO 27001 readiness is being held back by 3 incomplete vendor assessments and 1 open policy review. At current velocity, these will not close before your target date."

Pam doesn't panic. She clicks into the ISO track, sees exactly what's open, and reassigns the vendor assessments to Denise with a 48-hour due date. She flags the policy review for herself. Then she exports a current-posture summary as a PDF and sends it to the customer with a note: "Formal report coming end of month — here's where we stand today."

The customer replies within the hour. The deal stays on track.

Forecasting: Getting Ahead of Problems

Vanta surfaces a proactive signal in Pam's feed. Not a control failure — a trajectory problem. The kind of thing that, without this view, she'd discover three days before the audit.

Vanta AI — Forward Signal
"Based on current velocity, Orbit's SOC 2 readiness may fall short of your target audit date. 8 controls have open findings with an average remediation time of 18 days. At this pace, 3 controls will likely remain open at audit start."

Pam clicks in and sees exactly which controls, who owns them, and their projected close dates. Two of the three belong to an engineering team that's been slow to respond. Vanta surfaces a suggested action.

Suggested action
Escalate to engineering lead, or defer with documented rationale.

She clicks escalate. Vanta generates a summary message: "3 open findings in your area are projected to impact Orbit's audit timeline. Can we align this week?" She sends it directly from the platform. The engineering lead responds within an hour.

Pam didn't firefight. She adjusted course six weeks early.

Red Alert: Something Goes Wrong

Tuesday morning. Pam opens Vanta and sees a red banner.

Critical Finding — Orbit
"12 user accounts with active credentials are mapped to former employees."

An integration with Orbit's identity provider had a sync failure three weeks ago. The stale accounts weren't caught until now. This is a live SOC 2 access management control failure.

Vanta immediately shows Pam the full picture: the affected control, the framework requirements tied to it, the accounts involved, and the recommended remediation path. It's already logged the finding and assigned remediation to Denise.

Pam acknowledges the alert and sends a quick note to Caesar: "We identified a stale access issue in Orbit — 12 accounts being revoked now. Remediation ETA 24 hours. I'll send an updated posture summary once resolved."

Vanta AI — Compliance Disclosure
"This finding will need to be disclosed in your next SOC 2 report. Here's the suggested disclosure language."

Pam closes her laptop for lunch. It's handled.

Any Surface: The Reporting Agent Goes With You

Caesar doesn't live in Vanta. He lives in Gemini, Slack, and whatever tool is open on his second monitor. And increasingly, he asks questions in those tools — not by switching apps, but by talking to the AI interface in front of him.

That's where the reporting agent matters.

He types a question in Gemini: "Where does our compliance program stand heading into the audit?" Gemini pulls from Vanta's MCP. But it doesn't return raw JSON — it returns the full Vanta reporting layer. Same health scores, same risk themes, same Ima identity. The viz is Vanta's. The data is Vanta's. ScoSeg is ambient

From M2 onward, every surface is ScoSeg-aware. Health scores, reports, Ima signals, and dataviz all recalculate based on scope and segment context. This isn't a feature — it's a design principle that applies to everything downstream.

The dataviz kit debuts in M2 inside My Program, raising the visual bar for the entire product. It expands with each milestone — more components, more surfaces, more reach. Build it once. Show up everywhere.
