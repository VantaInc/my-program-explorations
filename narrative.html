03 — What we're building

Five bets, one coherent experience

Each of these is a distinct surface, but they're designed to compound. Together they form a program intelligence layer that lives across Vanta.

My Program view

The home base. A single view that orients you on your entire program — health, momentum, what needs you today.

This isn't a dashboard. It's a command center for your program — opinionated about what matters, quiet about what doesn't. Every widget earns its place.

Dataviz kit

A consistent visual language for data that lives everywhere Vanta does — not just in reports.

Right now, every surface in Vanta shows data differently. Numbers, tables, color-coded pills — but no visual system tying it together. The dataviz kit is a shared component library for data storytelling that permeates the whole product.

Smart insights

Reporting — data on ice

Structured data that can generate real reports — not exports, not screenshots.

Today's "reports" are PDFs of what your screen looks like. Data on ice is the concept that your program data is always structured, always ready — so when you need a QBR deck, a board update, or an audit package, it assembles itself from live data rather than getting manually assembled from stale exports.

Forecasting

Planning tools for where your program is going — not just where it is.

Compliance isn't a snapshot — it's a trajectory. Forecasting gives practitioners the tools to plan ahead, model scenarios, and communicate confidence about where the program will be at audit time, not just where it is today.

04 — Rituals

The system that knows your rhythms

The most valuable thing a tool can do is disappear into your workflow. Rituals is the idea that the system learns your cadences and shapes itself around them.

Not just calendar events — but the texture of how you actually work. The meetings you prepare for, the stakeholders you report to, the specific things each of them cares about.

Institutional

Org-wide moments

QBRs, board meetings, audit windows, executive briefings — the recurring events that every program practitioner prepares for, regardless of their specific context.

Ritual examples
QBR prep, board deck assembly, audit window countdown.
Personal

Your specific rhythms

Every Tuesday you sync with Caesar. He wants a quick health update, one risk to watch, and anything that needs his sign-off. The system knows this — and has it ready before you open the meeting.
